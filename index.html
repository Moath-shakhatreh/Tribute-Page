Contribution of Jabir ibn Hayyan in chemistry


1-Who is Jabir ibn Hayyan ?

Jaber bin Hayyan bin Abdullah al-Kufi al-Azdi, an Arab Muslim scholar. He excelled in the sciences of chemistry, astronomy, engineering, mineralogy, philosophy, medicine and pharmacy, and Jabir bin Hayyan is the first in history to use chemistry practically,He was born according to the most famous narrations in the year 101 AH / 721 AD.

2-His contributions:

  • He is credited with inventing and preparing a number of acids, including sulfuric acid, which he called vitriol oil. The total contributions attributed to Ibn Hayyan amounted to nearly 3,000 manuscripts.

  • Among the most important scientific contributions of Jaber in chemistry is the introduction of the experimental approach to chemistry, and he is the inventor of alkali known in modern chemistry terminology by its Arabic name (Alkali), and silver water. He introduced the two elements of experiment and lab in chemistry, and recommended the accuracy of research, reliance on experiment, and patience to do it.

  • Some of Ibn Hayyan's achievements:

    1. Discover "caustic soda" or tar (NaOH). First one to make golden water.
    2. He was the first to introduce the method of separating gold from silver by dissolving it with acids. It is the prevailing way to this day.
    3. The one who discover the nitric acid.
    4. The one who discover the nitric acid.
    5. The first to discover hydrochloric acid.
    6. He believed in autogenesis.
    7. He added two jewels to the four elements of Greece, namely (sulfur and mercury), and the Arabs added a third jewel, which is (salt).
